## The core trade-off: "Recruiters only" vs. public #OpenToWork

LinkedIn offers two visibility modes for Open-to-Work. They are not equivalent, and the right choice changes over time.

### Option A — "Recruiters only" (the green frame is hidden)

- Shows your job preferences **only to LinkedIn Recruiter seat-holders**, not to your network or your current employer's view of your profile.
- LinkedIn states it takes steps to hide this from recruiters at your own company, but **it is not a guarantee** — overlap and data lag happen.
- Best when you are **still employed and want discretion.**

### Option B — Public "#OpenToWork" green photo frame

- Adds the green "#OpenToWork" ring to your photo, visible to **everyone**.
- Maximizes inbound and signals availability loudly. Studies and LinkedIn's own data suggest it increases recruiter contact.
- The cost: it is **public**, including to colleagues and your current employer. It can read as "actively leaving" while you are still in seat.

### Recommendation: discretion now → public closer to availability

Because Mike is **employed through May 2026**:

1. **Now through ~April 2026:** Use **Option A (Recruiters only).** Get the inbound benefit without broadcasting a departure while still in seat. Pair this with value-first posting (see `activity-posts.md`) so momentum builds quietly.
2. **~May–June 2026 (as availability nears, and once it's appropriate relative to his employer):** Switch to **Option B (public green frame)** to maximize reach right before the **July 2026** start. This is also when availability-forward posts (Post 1 in `activity-posts.md`) should lead the mix.
3. Either way, keep the underlying **job preferences** filled in from day one — Option A already feeds them to recruiters.

> Judgment call: the timing of any public switch is Mike's to make relative to his own notice/employer situation. The default here is "discreet while in seat, loud near availability."
