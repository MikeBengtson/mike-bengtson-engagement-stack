# LinkedIn — Recommendations

Who to ask, what each recommendation should reinforce, and ready-to-send request notes.

**Do not invent names.** The targets below are **relationship types** — map each to a real
person from your own network before sending. Aim for 3–5 recommendations that, together,
cover both halves of the positioning: **hands-on builder** and **org-scaling leader**.

Best practice: ask the recommender to (1) name a specific project or moment, (2) name a
result or number if they can, and (3) say what you're like to work with. Vague praise reads
as filler — specifics read as proof.

## Target recommender types (by relationship → competency to reinforce)

| # | Recommender type (relationship) | Competency their rec should reinforce | Why this person |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Fidelity skip-level / senior leader** (the exec above you on Brokerage AI / AIDE) | Strategic engineering leadership against nine-figure KPIs; standing up the agentic-development practice org-wide | Speaks to scope, judgment, and trust at the VP/Director altitude. |
| 2 | **Fidelity peer leader** (a fellow chapter/squad or product/eng peer on AIDE) | Cross-org execution and collaboration; growing the delivery org (14→31) and shipping the high-impact servicing features | Peer credibility — "what he's like to partner with," not just to report to. |
| 3 | **Fidelity direct report** (an engineer who built on AIDE / the agentic pipeline under you) | Hands-on technical leadership; setting standards (Claude Code, Codex, SpecKit, MCP, code graphs) and pairing in the codebase | Proves the "builder + leader at once" claim from the ground up. |
| 4 | **Amobee architect / engineering peer** (Back-end Architecture or a Social Channels teammate) | Deep architecture chops — Amobee Marketplace, Social 2.0 microservices, OAuth/identity, Kubernetes migration | Establishes the pre-AI technical foundation and longevity. |
| 5 | **ACI Worldwide executive** (a leader from the Service Delivery Management era) | Running a large global org against revenue ($300M SaaS, 18-person team) and the on-prem→SaaS transition | Business-and-revenue leadership signal for senior/exec-track readers. |

Coverage check: items 1–3 prove **current agentic-AI leadership** (most important), item 4 proves
**technical depth**, item 5 proves **scaled business leadership**. Prioritize 1, 3, and 4 if you
can only land three — they cover leadership, hands-on engineering, and architecture.

## Recommendation-request note templates (≤300 chars each)

LinkedIn lets you add a note when you request a recommendation. Keep it short, name the
competency you want, and make it easy to say yes. Personalize the bracketed parts.

### Template A — for a leader/skip-level (reinforces strategic leadership)

> Hi [Name] — I'm refreshing my LinkedIn and would value a recommendation from you. If you're open to it, a few lines on the agentic-AI practice and AIDE work we drove — scope, judgment, results — would mean a lot. Happy to return the favor anytime. Thank you!

_(285 chars)_

### Template B — for a direct report (reinforces hands-on technical leadership)

> Hi [Name] — would you be willing to write me a short LinkedIn recommendation? Something on what it was like building the agentic pipeline together — the standards we set and the hands-on side — would be perfect. No rush, and glad to write you one in return. Thanks!

_(263 chars)_

### Template C — for a peer/architect (reinforces architecture + collaboration)

> Hi [Name] — I'd really appreciate a LinkedIn recommendation from you. A couple of specific lines on what we built together at [Amobee/Fidelity] and how I am to partner with would be ideal. Totally understand if you're swamped — and happy to reciprocate. Thank you!

_(261 chars)_

## Tips

- **Stagger the asks** — 2–3 per week, not all at once, so they don't feel mass-mailed.
- **Offer to reciprocate** — every template does; it materially raises reply rate.
- **Lightly steer the content** — naming the competency (above) helps the writer and keeps the set balanced; you can also offer a one-line "feel free to mention X" in the note.
- **Sequence with Featured** — a recommendation that names gemba-core or AIDE pairs well with those Featured items.
