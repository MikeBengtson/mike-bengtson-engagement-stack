# Positioning

## One-Line Positioning

AI engineering leader who scales platforms to millions of users and personally ships the agentic-AI toolchains that make engineering organizations dramatically faster.

## Short Bio

Mike Bengtson is an AI engineering leader with two decades building and scaling production platforms. He is equally at home leading multi-squad organizations against nine-figure KPIs and pairing with engineers in the codebase through agentic tooling — Claude Code, Codex, MCP servers, and RAG. At Fidelity he led Brokerage's agentic-AI transformation: standing up the enterprise's agentic-development practice (including a code-generation pipeline that cut some delivery cycles up to ~6×) and engineering the $175M AIDE servicing platform, after first rescuing a stalled data platform and scaling it from 0 to 7M+ users. Earlier, he architected industry-first AdTech and payments products at Amobee, ACI Worldwide, S1, and Mosaic Software. He is a builder and a leader at once — setting engineering standards, scaling teams, and writing code with the same toolchains he asks his org to adopt. He created and maintains gemba-core, an open-source framework for parallel agentic development.

## Best Fit Work

- VP / Director of Engineering roles where the mandate is to operationalize agentic / AI-assisted development across an organization.
- Hands-on engineering leadership that pairs org-building (hiring, scaling squads, SDLC and release governance) with real time in the codebase.
- Standing up an agentic-development practice end to end: standards, tooling (Claude Code, Codex, MCP, SpecKit, code-graph intelligence), RAG/MCP knowledge layers, evals, and guardrails.
- Rescuing, stabilizing, and scaling production platforms — especially under deadline or after a stalled launch — toward large, measurable KPIs.
- Cloud/platform modernization on AWS with microservices, event-driven architecture, and Kubernetes.
- Fractional or advisory engagements advising leadership on AI engineering strategy, agentic adoption, and developer-velocity transformation.

## Not A Fit

- Pure people-manager or program-manager roles with no expectation of staying technical or hands-on.
- Individual-contributor-only roles with no leadership, standards-setting, or team-scaling scope.
- Organizations not interested in adopting agentic / AI-assisted development practices.
- Early-stage roles seeking a single full-stack builder with no team to lead or org to shape (unless explicitly a founding-leader mandate).
