# Engagement Models

This file describes the engagement models Mike Bengtson is open to, and what a strong version of each looks like for an AI engineering leader. Mike is open to **all four**. This is public-safe; compensation specifics live in the private overlay (`private/compensation.private.yaml`).

The five headings below preserve this stack's standard model taxonomy. Mike's four real-world models (full-time, fractional / part-time leadership, contract / consulting, advisory / board) map onto them as noted.

## Full-Time Employment

Maps to: **full-time (FTE)** — *open.*

A good full-time engagement is a VP / Director of Engineering role where the mandate is to operationalize agentic / AI-assisted development across an organization, paired with real time in the codebase. Mike does his best work when he both shapes the org — hiring, scaling squads, setting SDLC and release governance, aligning roadmap across stakeholders — and ships with the same toolchain he asks the org to adopt (Claude Code, Codex, MCP servers, RAG, code-graph intelligence, evals, and guardrails). Ideal scope includes standing up an agentic-development practice end to end, modernizing a platform on AWS, or rescuing and scaling a production system toward large, measurable KPIs.

A weak version is a pure people-/program-management seat with no expectation of staying hands-on, or a role where "AI" is a label on legacy work with no real adoption mandate.

## Hourly Contract

Maps to: **contract / consulting (time-based)** — *open.*

A good contract engagement is a scoped, time-based consulting effort where Mike brings senior AI-engineering leverage to a specific problem: standing up an agentic-development practice, building a RAG + MCP knowledge layer, defining the agentic SDLC and guardrails, or stabilizing and scaling a system under deadline. Works best with a clear statement of work, defined outcomes, and decision rights over tooling and process. Rate basis and terms are handled privately (see `private/compensation.private.yaml`); the public rubric notes that a contracting premium exists without revealing the number.

A weak version is open-ended staff-augmentation with no clear outcome, no autonomy, or low-agency maintenance work that does not build on his strengths.

## Fixed-Bid Project

Maps to: **contract / consulting (scoped / outcome build)** — *open.*

A good fixed-bid engagement is a well-defined build or transformation with a concrete deliverable and acceptance criteria — for example, an agentic code-generation pipeline, an MCP/RAG knowledge layer over institutional docs and API contracts, a prototype-to-production AI feature, or a targeted platform-modernization slice. Fixed-bid works when scope, milestones, and definition of done are clear enough to price and deliver against. Mike has a track record of shipping architecture-led builds on time and on budget (e.g., the industry-first Amobee Marketplace storefront).

A weak version has fuzzy or shifting scope, no acceptance criteria, or a deliverable that depends on factors outside his control.

## Advisory / Fractional

Maps to: **fractional / part-time leadership** and **advisory / board** — *both open, and both preferred.*

This covers two distinct but related shapes:

- **Fractional / part-time leadership.** Acting as a fractional head of engineering or AI-engineering leader for an organization that needs senior leverage without a full-time hire — owning the agentic-adoption roadmap, raising the engineering bar, mentoring leads, and setting standards on a defined weekly cadence. A good version has a clear charter, real decision rights, and a manageable meeting load.
- **Advisory / board.** Advising founders and leadership on AI engineering strategy, agentic adoption, developer-velocity transformation, and platform scaling — as a technical advisor, board member, or expert. A good version is narrow in scope with a low, predictable meeting load and clear expectations.

Weekly capacity for fractional and advisory work is **8–16 hours/week**. A weak version is an open-ended "advisor" title with unbounded asks, a heavy meeting load, or no real influence on decisions.

## Assignment / Consignment / Outcome-Based

Maps to: **not offered.**

Mike does not take assignment-, consignment-, or pure outcome-/success-fee-based engagements. He works under salary, hourly, retainer, and fixed-bid structures only. Route any scoped-outcome build through fixed-bid (or contract / consulting) on an accepted comp structure, or decline — do not accept success-fee or consignment terms.
