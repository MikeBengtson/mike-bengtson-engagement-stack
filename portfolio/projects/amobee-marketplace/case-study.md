# Case Study

> Commercial product, proprietary. Described from the resume of record; no public artifact remains to link.

## Context

Amobee (a Singtel company) ran a SaaS AdTech platform unifying mobile, social, video, and display advertising across consolidated planning, back-end tools, and analytics. Buying advertising inventory in that world was a planner's workflow, not a shopping experience.

## Problem

Make advertising inventory feel like a product catalog — let users browse and "shop" inventory the way they would on a consumer storefront — and do it as a delivered, on-budget commercial product.

## Constraints

- Novel product pattern with no off-the-shelf blueprint ("industry-first").
- Commercial deadline and budget.
- Hybrid onshore/offshore delivery team.

## Approach

- **Architected** Amobee Marketplace and **owned the stack selection**: GraphQL for the API surface, AOP/annotation-driven services on the back end, and React on the front end.
- **Led delivery** with a hybrid on/offshore team, driving the product from concept to a shipped storefront.

## Outcome

Delivered an industry-first "Amazon-style" storefront for advertising inventory **on time and on budget** — presenting ad inventory as shoppable products inside the Amobee platform.
